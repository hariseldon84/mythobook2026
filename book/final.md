# Kurukshetra: Error 404
*(Alternative Titles: Hastinapur Heights, The Noida Yuga, Mahabharata 2.0: The Glitch, Pandavas & Co.)*

## 📖 Plot Introduction
Rahul Sharma, 34, a mildly useless software engineer from Noida, is test-driving his eccentric uncle’s time machine—a customized Maruti 800 running on two Amazon Alexas and a JioFiber router. There is a glitch. Rahul lands in Hastinapur, 3200 BCE, right as the Pandavas and Kauravas are returning from Dronacharya's Gurukul. There’s a side effect to this temporal jump: because Rahul’s biological age can only increment when the calendar switches from 2026 to 2027, and he is stuck in BCE, he effectively stops aging. He becomes the perpetually 34-year-old millennial intern of the Mahabharata.

Rahul stumbles into the grand arena where the princes are showcasing their martial skills. Immediately, he realizes history is *wildly* different from the mythological TV serials. He befriends Sahadeva, who possesses the curse of knowing the future but nobody listens to him. Sahadeva is a deeply depressed, misunderstood psychic who spends his days humming Mohammed Rafi songs in his head. 

Rahul tries to prevent the great war, but realizes the biggest obstacle isn't the rivalry between the cousins—it's **Vyasa**, a struggling author working for *Ganesha Media Ltd.* who desperately needs a bloody, epic war to secure his publishing legacy. Rahul accidentally fixes the family rivalry by introducing them to pizzas and corporate rock-dancing, sending Vyasa into a manic frenzy. Now Rahul, along with his brilliant new accidental fiancé Draupadi, must navigate a corporate Shakuni, an Italian-styled Indraprastha, Vyasa's PR sabotage rumors, and random check-ins from a very chill, all-knowing Krishna, all to stop the world's greatest epic from becoming the world's most unnecessary bloodbath.

## 🎭 The Cast of Characters
*   **Rahul Sharma:** Protagonist. Useless, tired, zero survival skills, completely immune to aging. Accidental fiancé to Draupadi.
*   **Sahadeva:** Rahul’s best friend. Sees the future, knows Bollywood lyrics, massively depressed because nobody takes his prophecies seriously. 
*   **Shakuni:** Holds a post-graduate certificate from the "Drona Institute of Awesomeness." An opportunistic corporate shark. Runs "MBHT (Mera Bachcha Hai Tu) Media" and trapped Rahul in a 5000-year non-compete clause.
*   **Vyasa:** The antagonist. A frustrated official war scribe working at Ganesha Media Ltd. who desperately needs a dramatic war for his upcoming book to sell and actively sabotages Rahul's peaceful vibe.
*   **Lord Ganesha:** Reclusive CEO of Ganesha Media Ltd., lives in Kailasha Mansion, possesses cross-timeline tech, and only grants meetings during ribbon-cutting inaugurations.
*   **Draupadi / Panchali:** Brilliant, politically savvy, and accidentally engaged to Rahul. She uses Rahul as her corporate puppet admin to run the Pandavas' empire behind the scenes, dodging the "married to five men" gossip column rumors.
*   **Krishna (Madhava):** Smirking, omniscient, completely unbothered. Casually drops by in chariots playing top-40 pop, acting as a highly amused Venture Capitalist.

## 🗺️ Scene-by-Scene Narrative & Chapter Outline

### Chapter 1: The Maruti Landing and The Arena
Rahul’s Maruti 800 crashes into a haystack near the Hastinapur graduation arena. He wanders in. Suyodhana (Duryodhana) is fighting Sahadeva. Shakuni claims every arrow Arjuna shoots is an HR violation. Everyone assumes Rahul is a Chief Entertainer because of his weird clothes.

### Chapter 2: The Prophet and the IT Guy
Rahul becomes Sahadeva’s assistant. Sahadeva reveals he knows everything—from the results of the Kurukshetra war to the ending of *Avengers: Endgame*. Sahadeva is thrilled to finally have someone to talk to about pop culture and avoid the family's brutal martial expectations.

### Chapter 3: "Chief Product Officer" of Hastinapur
Rahul accidentally unites the cousins by inventing the "Bhim-Chakra" (pizza) and "Bhim Cola". He teaches Dushasana and Bhima how to do a Bhangra-Rock-and-Roll fusion dance, completely erasing the toxic rivalry and turning the palace into a frat house. Vyasa watches in horror as his dark epic becomes a sitcom.

### Chapter 4: The Start-Up Pitch & The 5000-Year Trap
Shakuni corners Rahul and Sahadeva. He pitches his startup "MBHT Media". 

### Chapter 5: The Deal, The Dakshina, and The Desi Chakravyuh
Through sleazy negotiation tactics, Shakuni gets Rahul to sign a 5,000-year non-compete clause in exchange for "Priority YouTube listing" (screaming orphans) and 1 million forced followers, legally trapping Rahul in corporate servitude until 1800 AD..
Drona asks the princes for Guru Dakshina: capturing King Drupad. Sahadeva bribes Drona out of going using 12-year-aged Somras. In Panchaal, the Princes get mocked and baited into a Chakravyuh. Rahul accidentally navigates the spinning maze by exploiting algorithm latency, does a silly victory dance, and Drupad, impressed by his non-violent genius, bets his daughter Draupadi's hand to him immediately.

### Chapter 6: The Accidental Groom & The PR Disaster
Rahul is fundamentally panicked by being engaged to Draupadi. Draupadi, however, loves the arrangement: she acts as the real "CEO", using Rahul as her harmless beta-male puppet to institute widespread administrative HR changes over the Pandavas. Furious at the lack of violence, Vyasa intentionally leaks the "married to five Pandava brothers" rumor on the palace WhatsApp group to aggressively ruin Rahul's life and inject scandalous PR back into his book.

### Chapter 7: The Ganesha Media Ltd. Meeting
Desperate to stop Vyasa's relentless character assassination, Rahul secures a 2-minute meeting with Lord Ganesha at a random chariot-inauguration ceremony. Ganesha's office is filled with futuristic tech. Ganesha imparts deep, humorous wisdom about not fighting the timeline, but agrees to temporarily block Vyasa’s Wi-Fi.

### Chapter 8: The Italian Indraprastha Real Estate Dispute
A bureaucratic dispute (instigated by Vyasa) forces the Pandavas to relocate to the barren Khandavprastha. Draupadi runs the relocation ops. Sahadeva brings in a fake Italian designer to build an incredibly sleek, modern Roman-style startup campus. They launch the new kingdom with a massive corporate mixer.

### Chapter 9: The Dice Game (Corporate Hostile Takeover)
Shakuni organizes the infamously tense Dice Game. But it's not for kingdoms—it's for aggressive corporate equity transfer of Indraprastha. Yudhishthira is losing badly at Hakuna's rigged "Truth or Dare" challenges. Rahul halts the corporate takeover by performing Hastinapur’s first Stand-Up Roast, distracting the entire royal court in a 4-hour laughter fit while Sahadeva actively shreds the equity contracts.

### Chapter 10: The Forced Sabbatical (Forest Exile)
Having lost the technical HR terms, the Pandavas are forced to endure a 13-year "corporate off-site" in the forest. Instead of suffering, Draupadi and Rahul turn it into an extreme agile bootcamp with team-building trust falls, vision-boarding, and daily 6-hour scrum stand-ups for Yudhishthira.

### Chapter 11: The Arrival of Madhava (VC Investor)
Krishna finally makes his physical entrance. Instead of a god, he acts like a charismatic 21st-century Venture Capitalist. He effortlessly mocks Rahul's past dating failures from 2026, hums current pop songs, and advises Draupadi on seed-funding expansions in the outer provinces.

### Chapter 12: The Dwarka VC Pitch & Peace Envoy
Seeking backup for their upcoming restructure against the Kauravas, Arjuna and Suyodhana race to Dwarka to pitch Krishna for capital. It plays out like two rival tech CEOs begging a VC. Krishna later visits Hastinapur with a terrible, 50-slide palm-leaf PowerPoint detailing why war is un-optimized for quarterly profits.

### Chapter 13: Takshaka's Threat (The Naga Hacker)
King Takshaka of the Nagas, enraged by a bad Yelp review on Shakuni's *Mama Ke Nushkhe* channel, threatens to nuke the region. By "nuke," he effectively hacks their irrigation system and completely blocks the chariot supply routes, acting as an ancient ransomware attack.

### Chapter 14: Kurukshetra: The Corporate Merger Strategy
Realizing they are facing a hostile takeover from Takshaka, the Kauravas and Pandavas quickly drop their beef and are forced to synergize. The "War" is actually a chaotic, highly disorganized corporate merger-style defense against the Naga forces.

### Chapter 15: The 'War' against Takshaka
The actual battlefield is mostly confused middle-management soldiers standing around while Arjuna, Suyodhana, and Rahul try to figure out who is the project lead on attacking the Naga lines. Bhima accidentally defeats an entire battalion while sleep-walking towards his pop-up battle catering stall.

### Chapter 16: The Inevitable Error 404 (Epilogue)
Vyasa, crying over his ruined violent epic, writes the massive bloodbath anyway, heavily embellishing everything so the final *Mahabharata* looks nothing like the goofy startup reality Rahul lived through. Rahul stays in the past, eternally a 34-year-old middle manager, working as Sahadeva's therapist, Draupadi's Admin assistant, and permanently ignoring Shakuni's aggressive LinkedIn requests.
