# Acknowledgements

Since long, the Mahabharata has been my absolute favorite epic. I have religiously watched every single version of the TV serials, and our family has even enthusiastically role-played some of the iconic characters in our living room. My wife, Himani, my son, Medhansh, and my daughter, Prisha, are avid fans of the monumental saga and its legendary heroes. 

However, as a fan, I always used to wonder: *Why can't two sets of cousins from the same royal family just sit down, grab a coffee, and establish a relaxed "cousin chill" vibe?* Did it *really* have to end in an earth-shattering war? 

*The Kuru Pivot: Debugging the Mahabharata* is my humble attempt to answer that exact question. It is an endeavor to inject modern corporate humor into the greatest epic of our age. 

Before we go any further, I want to make one thing absolutely clear: I have zero intention of hurting anyone's sentiments or manipulating our sacred narratives. I hold profound, unyielding respect for the original Mahabharata, its legendary characters, and the divine wisdom it imparts. 

I am a deeply religious and spiritual man. My daily routine revolves around my *Puja* and prayers. I have utter dedication and devotion to Lord Krishna, and I always feel His guiding presence whenever I undertake a new initiative or find myself in trouble. By no means would I ever think, say, or write anything that disrespects my devotion, or the profound devotion that millions hold for our beloved Gods. In fact, I sometimes like to believe that Lord Krishna possesses a fantastic sense of humor, and that He might just read this book up in Vaikuntha, sharing a hearty laugh over my terrible corporate jokes with Goddess Lakshmi! 

Having survived a very tragic incident in my own life, I firmly believe that I am here today solely because of divine intervention. It is my unwavering faith in Lord Shiva that gave me a renewed purpose. It is through my prayers to Lord Ganesha that I seek blessings whenever I start something new. It is my blind reliance on Lord Hanuman that makes me feel safe and protected in this chaotic world, and it is my complete devotion to Lord Vishnu that keeps me strong and blessed every single day. I would never intentionally write anything to cast even a shadow of disrespect upon them. 

That being said, this book is entirely a work of satirical fiction. Please do not use it as a historical or mythological reference! If you want to know the true, magnificent history of Bharata, I highly recommend consulting the actual epic. If you just want to lighten your mood, laugh at some bad HR policies, and watch the Kauravas struggle with spreadsheet management, you are in the right place. I sincerely apologize in advance to anyone whose feelings might be inadvertently hurt by this—my only goal was to make you smile. 

I highly suggest that you do not overthink the plot—or rather, try not to think too much at all! Just buckle up and enjoy the roller-coaster ride. I have personally laughed out loud writing this completely erroneous, deliberately incorrect, and completely messed-up version of the Mahabharata, and I hope you enjoy reading it just as much as I enjoyed writing it. 

I want to vehemently thank my beautiful daughter, Prisha Arora, and my wonderful son, Medhansh, for patiently listening to all my chaotic stories and providing excellent suggestions to improve the plot. I also want to thank my incredible wife, Himani, for tolerating my "not-so-funny" jokes for years and constantly supporting me while writing this book. I promise I'll stop making startup puns at the dinner table... eventually.
